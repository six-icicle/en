Why Rose Pine reads great and Washi BEFORE doesn't

  • Rose Pine follows the lifted-bg recipe: each step from --bg → --tile → --tile-2 goes UP in lightness. Surfaces feel "lit from within."
  • Washi BEFORE: --tile-2 goes DARKER than --tile. Hovers go DOWN, not UP. Tile-head bar reads as recessed instead of elevated.
  • Rose Pine's --ink jump is dramatic — 60% L → 90% L (huge gap). The tile-name in the active head really pops. Washi's --ink only jumps 30% → 8% (similar magnitude). Pulling to 5% gives that same dramatic "this is the loudest text" feel.

How the new --ok and --warn were derived (math)

  • --warn follows rose-pine's recipe: hue −19° from accent, sat +10pp, lightness −8pp.
    Washi accent #bc002d = HSL(346° 100% 37%) → shifted = HSL(327° 100% 29%) = #94004f (deep magenta-rose, sibling to shu-iro)
  • --ok = dodger-blue territory tuned for cream readability: HSL(212° 65% 47%) = #2a78c8. Confident-AI blue without RGB-cheap.
  • Light tones stripped of warmth: subtle cool tint (HSL(218° 16% L)) instead of warm cream (HSL(36° 30% L)). Reads as "lit room" not "tea-stained paper."
