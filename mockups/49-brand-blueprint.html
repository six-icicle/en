00 Essence what en is, in one breath

en (縁 — connection, fate, the central point that links) is a tea-ceremony aesthetic for software: quiet surfaces, restrained color, deliberate motion, and a deep respect for the user's attention. The default is calm. Loudness is reserved for moments that earn it — an alert, an active state, a freshly-summoned action.

The aesthetic borrows from Japanese visual language: muted earth pigments rather than saturated screen colors, hairline strokes rather than heavy borders, lifted backgrounds that feel "lit from within" rather than "ink on void." Names lean toward Japanese terms (kanagawa, hinoki, kindle, kodou) when they fit naturally — never as costume.

The brand is for tools that respect the operator. Where most app design fights for attention, en is what's left over when you stop fighting.

Three principles

  • Quiet by default. Every surface starts subdued. Loudness is rationed and meaningful.
  • Match the family. If a pattern exists, the next variant copies its full contract — tokens, defaults, motion, edge cases. Drift is the enemy.
  • Restraint is the design. A removed element, a desaturated accent, a smaller number — these are the choices, not omissions.

01 Typography three faces, locked roles

Three typefaces, no substitutions. Each role is locked.

display — Outfit 600

Wordmarks, primary nameplates only. Geometric, slightly humanist, reads as quiet authority. Used at 16–56px depending on context. Never used for body text — at body sizes Outfit's open apertures feel airy in a way that fights the body face.

sans — Space Grotesk 500

All UI body text — buttons, labels, paragraphs, descriptions. Body weight is 500, not 400; en's body text is sturdier than typical sans default. The slightly distinctive terminals (the a, g, t) give the UI character without needing decorative chrome.

mono — JetBrains Mono 400/500

Used in two registers: terminal output (xterm, code) at 400, and UI metadata (status text, kbd legends, timestamps, percentages, section labels) at 400-500. Mono in chrome should always feel like a label, not a paragraph — uppercase + 0.10–0.18em letter-spacing for label use.

scale

Body 16px nominal, scaled by --fz (0.7–1.6). Chrome labels 10–11px. Buttons 11–13px. The scale is intentionally narrow — en doesn't have h1/h2/h3 hierarchies because it's an app shell, not a document.

02 Color & Themes eight palettes, four pairs

en has four theme families, each with two variants:

  • default (high-contrast) — deeper bg, brighter ink, more saturated accent. The "crisp" reading.
  • -soft (lifted) — bg pulled up not down, ink tonally related to bg, accent restrained. Feels "lit from within" rather than "crisp on void."

The lifted recipe is the soul of en. When in doubt, lean lifted. The default rose-pine-soft palette is the canonical reference — every other theme is tuned to that target feel.

Color tokens (per theme)

--bg page background the deepest surface; vignette + texture render against this
--tile primary surface cards, popovers, modals; one step above bg
--tile-2 elevated surface hover states, internal headers; one step above tile
--line hairline divider subtle internal separators
--line-2 visible border button outlines, popover edges
--ink primary text titles, prominent values
--ink-2 body text paragraphs, secondary values, button labels
--ink-3 label text caps section labels, captions, descriptions
--ink-4 disabled / placeholder inactive elements, empty status dots
--accent brand color interactive icons, focus rings, key glyphs
--accent-glow accent at low alpha hover backgrounds, soft halos
--ok / --warn status colors live (ok), needs / error (warn); per theme, never hardcoded

03 Spacing, Motion, Elevation disciplined tokens, two durations

spacing

Body padding/gap/margin in interactive chrome scales with --fz via calc(Npx * var(--fz)). Borders, hairlines, blur radii, fixed widths/heights, breakpoints, and border-radius do NOT scale. The rule: if the text inside scales, the chrome around it should breathe; visual constants stay constant.

motion

Two durations, period.

  • --dur-fast: 120ms — every micro-interaction (hover, focus, active, popover open, modal fade).
  • --dur-slow: 250ms — page-level transitions only (theme switch body fade).

Animations (alert pulses, ambient drift, decorative) are tuned per-effect (0.9s–28s). They are not durations — they are choreography. Each must have a prefers-reduced-motion: reduce opt-out.

elevation

en uses frosted glass, not drop shadows. The vocabulary is:

  • Inset 1px highlight on top edge (rgba(255,255,255,0.08)) — gives the surface a "lit-from-above" quality.
  • Backdrop-filter blur(20px) saturate(180%) on translucent surfaces.
  • Hairline 1px border with top edge slightly brighter than sides.
  • Soft outer shadow only for popovers and modals — never for inline buttons.

z-index

Three documented tiers:

  • 9997–9999 — global decorative overlays (texture, vignette, particles).
  • 50–100 — chrome (popovers 50, modals 100).
  • 1–6 — tile-internal effects (xterm, alert overlays).

04 Component patterns how building blocks behave

nav frosted pill

The signature en chrome element. 30×30 square with 9px radius. Frosted-glass surface (see elevation recipe). Inner glyph in var(--accent), sized 14×14 max. On hover: top-edge highlight brightens, accent ring forms. On press: scale(0.97), inset shadow flips to suggest pressed-in. Use for any nav action that opens a popover or fires a single command.

+ ↻ ● ▦ Aa

popover

Opens below trigger with 8px gap. Uses var(--tile) bg + 1px var(--line-2) border + inset 1px var(--accent) ring + 32px soft shadow. Open animation: fade + 4px translateY, 120ms. Click-outside dismisses. Escape dismisses.

modal

Center-of-screen on a rgba(0,0,0,0.55) backdrop with backdrop-blur(2px). Frame matches popover (tile bg, accent ring, deep shadow). Escape cancels; Enter confirms (when there's a primary action). Focus is trapped on the primary button on open.

alert state (tile.needs)

When a session needs the user, the tile escalates via one of 10 alert styles (kodou, kokyu, hotaru, samurai, sandangiri, tatewari, sakura-rain, ninja, shuriken, andon). Every style is driven by var(--accent) so it auto-themes. Each style honors prefers-reduced-motion — falling back to a soft static halo.

texture overlays

en supports 14 texture layers — 12 static (grain, scanlines, dots, sakura, seigaiha, topography, pluses, yagasuri, brush, bokeh, bamboo) and 2 animated rains (sakura-rain, shuriken-rain). All driven by --tex-amt (0.2–2.0) for global intensity. Textures are decorative atmosphere, never functional state.

brand mark

Torii gate SVG (4 strokes, currentColor) + "en" wordmark in Outfit 600. The torii sits before the wordmark, sized to match cap-height. Color is always var(--accent) — retints with theme automatically.

05 Voice & Copy how en sounds

Lowercase by default. Restrained verbs. Japanese terms only when the meaning fits naturally — never as decoration. Ration exclamation marks (zero is the default). Status text reads as observation, not announcement.

verb choices

en's verbs lean ceremonial-quiet. Where a typical app says create, open, start, en often picks something with weight: kindle, summon, begin. Not always — if the natural English word is correct, use it. The rule: pick the verb that respects the user's attention and the act being named.

examples

  • kindle one — start a new session (lighting a lantern; ties to the andon/torii motifs)
  • kindling… — session spawning (transition state)
  • no sessions — empty state (observation, not "Get started!" cheerfulness)
  • focus — current selection (used as a noun in foot bar)

kbd legends

Mono caps. ⌘ N with a space (the keys are visually distinct units). esc and ↵ in lowercase mono.
